Car sales have experienced significant fluctuations over the past few years, influenced by various economic, technological, and environmental factors. The global market has seen a shift towards electric and hybrid vehicles, driven by growing environmental concerns and government regulations aimed at reducing carbon emissions. Consumer preferences are also evolving, with an increasing demand for advanced safety features, connectivity, and autonomous driving capabilities. The COVID-19 pandemic initially disrupted production and supply chains, causing a temporary decline in sales. However, the market has shown resilience, rebounding as economies recover and as manufacturers adapt by improving online sales platforms and offering more flexible purchasing options. Additionally, incentives such as tax breaks and subsidies for electric vehicles have spurred interest and growth in this segment.
